## The Danish preposition "med" ##

**MED** Occurs with full range of complements.

  * PAROLE compounds: med\_hensyn\_til, med\_rette
  * Special constructions: [+ part + pobj](Verb.md)
    * Den skrumper ind **med** 50%.
    * De holdt op **med** at synge.
    * De dirigerede rundt **med** ham.
> > ![http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-29.png](http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-29.png)
  * Fixed expressions
    * sammen **med** ham
> > ![http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-30.png](http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-30.png)
    * i forbindelse **med** festen [structure as "i stedet for ham"](same.md)
> > ![http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-31.png](http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-31.png)
  * Adverbial uses (tagged as RG)
    * "med" can be used as a particle with many verbs, including spille, synge, tage, skulle, være, følge, komme, and have.
> > ![http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-32.png](http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-32.png)
> > > When "med" occurs as a complement to "være", it could alternatively be analyzed as pred. However, "med" does not occur with all copula verbs, which speaks against the pred analysis:
        * ??de synes med
        * **de blev med
    * As a particle "med" can cooccur with prepositional complements:
      * De sang med på omkvædet.
> > > ![http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-33.png](http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-33.png)
> > > > Alternatively, "med" could be (re)analyzed as a preposition and "på omkvædet" as a dependent (pobj) of "med".

> > > ![http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-34.png](http://copenhagen-dependency-treebank.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/figs/prep-34.png)
> > > > This would require that we allow (at least some) prepositions to take prepositional complements, which is otherwise not allowed.**
